# Phase 0 — Customer Discovery Interview Kit

**Goal:** 10 interviews with EU mid-market IT/security buyers by end of month 3.
**Framework:** [Mom Test](https://www.momtestbook.com/) — ask about past behavior, never about hypothetical preference.

---

## Sourcing plan (week 1–4)

Build a list of **30 prospects** in your target ICP. 3:1 ratio because cold conversion is brutal.

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator filters

- **Job title:** "IT Director" OR "Head of IT" OR "CISO" OR "Chief Information Security Officer" OR "IT Asset Manager" OR "Head of People Operations"
- **Company headcount:** 51–500
- **Industry:** Financial Services, Insurance, Legal Services, Hospitals & Healthcare, Information Technology & Services, Computer Software, Management Consulting
- **Country:** United Kingdom, Ireland, Netherlands, Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland
- **Posted on LinkedIn in last 90 days** — proves they're active, not a dead account

### Channel mix (run in parallel)

| Channel | Effort | Hit rate | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn Sales Nav cold outreach | 30 sends → expect 3–5 calls | ~10–15% reply, ~30% of replies → call | €99 trial |
| Personal network 2nd/3rd degree | Ask 5 trusted people for 1 intro each | ~80% reply when warm | €0 |
| Paid intros from EU ITAD consultants | Find 2–3 on LinkedIn, offer €100/qualified intro | ~50% conversion | €500–1000 |
| ITAM/IT communities (Bitkom, ICTU, r/sysadmin EU) | Post a "looking for input" thread | Variable, slow | €0 |

## Outreach message (LinkedIn / email)

Don't pitch. Don't sell. **Frame as research, not sales.**

```
Hi [name],

I'm researching how EU mid-market IT teams handle device offboarding under GDPR — specifically what happens to laptops when employees leave or upgrade.

I'm not selling anything. I'm 1 of 10 IT/security leaders I want to talk to before deciding whether there's a real product to build here.

20 minutes this month? I'll share what the other 9 say (anonymized) — usually the most useful part for the people I talk to.

[Your name]
```

### Variations

- **Replace "IT teams" with "security teams"** for CISOs.
- **Replace "device offboarding" with "asset disposal compliance"** for compliance officers.
- **Add a 1-line LinkedIn-bio reference** ("saw your post about NIS2 last week —") if they're active.

### Follow-up rhythm

- Day 0: initial message
- Day 5: 1-line bump ("hi [name], any thoughts on the above?")
- Day 12: final attempt, share a useful link, then drop

After 3 messages with no reply, leave them alone. Aggressive follow-up burns the brand.

## Interview script (20 minutes)

Open with: *"Thanks — quick reminder: I'm researching, not pitching. I'll spend most of the time asking you about your world. If at the end you have time, I'll share a 60-second sketch of what I'm thinking, but only if you ask."*

### Section 1 — Past behavior (12 minutes, the meat)

Ask all of these. Don't lead, don't fill silence, write down their **exact words**.

1. **"Walk me through the last time an employee left your company. What happened to their laptop?"**
2. **"Roughly how many devices left your company last year? What did you do with them — sold, refurbished, donated, landfilled?"**
3. **"Have you ever been asked by an employee if they could buy their old laptop? What did you say? Why?"**
4. **"What did you spend on ITAD / disposal / refurb last year? Was the result worth it?"**
5. **"Walk me through your data wiping process. Who certifies it? What happens if it fails an audit?"**
6. **"Tell me about your worst experience with offboarding or decommissioning a device. What went wrong?"**

### Section 2 — Magnitude check (4 minutes)

7. **"On a scale of 1 to 10, how much of a problem is this for you compared to the other things on your plate?"**
8. **"If a magic wand made this 10× easier, what would you stop doing? What would that be worth to you in a year?"**

### Section 3 — Concept test (only if they have time, ≤4 min)

Only after the past-behavior questions. Don't ask "would you buy this." Ask:

9. **"If a tool existed that let your employees buy their own devices at fair prices, with GDPR-compliant wiping built in — what would have to be true for you to try it for one quarter?"**
10. **"Who else in the company would weigh in on this decision? Procurement? Finance? Legal?"**

### Close

- "Who else should I be talking to? Anyone in your network who'd find this conversation interesting?" *(referrals are gold)*
- "Mind if I follow up in a few weeks if I have a working prototype?"
- Send a thank-you within 24 hours summarizing their 3 most interesting points.

## What "validated" looks like at end of Phase 0

Three concrete artifacts:

1. **Aggregated problem statement in customers' own words** — not yours, theirs. The phrases that 3+ people independently used.
2. **Frequency-ranked objections list** — every reason they said no, sorted by how often it came up.
3. **A count of "would pilot if it existed":** ≥4 of 10 = green light to Phase 1. <4 = wedge is wrong, reassess.

## Common failure modes (don't do these)

| Trap | Why it kills validation |
|---|---|
| Talking to friends / fellow founders | They want to support you. They'll lie politely. |
| Asking "would you buy this?" | Hypothetical questions get hypothetical answers. Past behavior only. |
| Pitching in the call | Once you pitch, they stop being honest. Hold until the end, only if they ask. |
| Not writing down exact words | You'll remember them as "they liked it." They didn't. They said something specific. The specific words are the gold. |
| Stopping at 5 interviews because 4 said yes | Talk to all 10. The 7th interview often surfaces an objection that changes everything. |
| Building features between interviews | The whole point is to build the *right* thing. Park feature work until Phase 1. |
